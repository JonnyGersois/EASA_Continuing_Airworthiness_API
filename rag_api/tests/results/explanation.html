Deferring a defect under the MEL when the aircraft is away from base and no certifying staff are available

ConditionSource
The defect does not hazard seriously flight safety – only non‑serious defects may be deferred.Chunk 1 (M.A.403 c)
The operator has a documented procedure (MOE) that specifies how to handle defects that require certifying staff who are not present – the procedure must identify which tasks can be certified by the available staff and how to defer the rest.Chunk 3 (MOE procedure)
The defect can be rectified within the time limits set in the MEL – the operator must plan to complete the work before the MEL‑specified deadline or obtain an authorised extension.Chunk 3 (MOE procedure)
The impact of the deferred work is assessed and recorded – the operator must evaluate whether the work can be postponed to a later check or time limit and document this decision.Chunk 4 (assessment of work not carried out)
The crew accepts the MEL deferment in the technical log – acceptance by the flight crew is required before the aircraft can depart.Chunk 4 (crew acceptance)
The deferred defect is recorded in the aircraft continuing airworthiness record system – all deferred defects must be logged.Chunk 1 (d)
The competent authority allows an over‑run of the MEL time limit only under specific conditions – if an extension is needed, it must be authorised by the authority.Chunk 5 (MEL time limitation overrun)

Summary

An operator may defer a defect while the aircraft is away from base and no certifying staff are available only if the defect is non‑serious, the operator has a clear MOE procedure that limits the scope of work to tasks that can be certified by the available staff, the defect can be rectified within the MEL‑specified time limit (or an authorised extension is obtained), the crew accepts the deferment in the technical log, and the defect is properly recorded. The competent authority may permit an extension of the MEL time limit under specific conditions, but this must be formally authorised.
