Continuing Airworthiness Management Responsibilities

The continuing airworthiness management system (CAMO) is organized around a set of clearly defined duties, authorities, and procedures. The key responsibilities are grouped into several categories:

CategoryWhat it CoversHow it is Implemented
Nominated Postholder & Group of Persons• The nominated postholder for continuing airworthiness must ensure that all maintenance is performed on time and to an approved standard.
• This person has corporate authority to finance and carry out all required activities.
• The group of persons (or the postholder alone in small operators) must cover all responsibilities described in Part‑M.		• The CAMO documents the extent of the postholder’s authority and the composition of the group.
• Duties and responsibilities of each member are elaborated in the CAMO exposition.
Manpower Resources & Training Policy• The CAMO must show that enough staff are dedicated to continuing airworthiness.
• Training policy must demonstrate that staff are qualified for their tasks.		• A table of full‑time and part‑time personnel (e.g., “AA”, “BB”, “CC”) is presented, showing total man‑hours.
• Training requirements are documented in the CAMO exposition.
Records & Documentation• Management of aircraft continuing‑airworthiness records and technical logs.
• Maintenance programme and Minimum Equipment List (MEL) procedures.
• Recording of flight hours and cycles.		• The CAMO describes the system used to manage records.
• It explains how the maintenance programme satisfies State of Registry requirements and how the MEL is issued, updated, and used.
• Procedures for recording hours/cycles are detailed.
Coordination & Oversight• The CAMO must coordinate all continuing‑airworthiness activities and ensure they are financed and performed to standard.• The exposition lists job functions that constitute the “group of persons” and shows coverage of Part‑M responsibilities.
Subcontracting• When tasks are subcontracted, the CAMO must control and monitor those arrangements.
• Subcontracted organisations must inform the CAMO of any changes that affect their ability to fulfil the contract.		• The CAMO has procedures for managing subcontracted tasks, including:
– Approval of subcontractors.
– Contracts that specify responsibilities, notification requirements, and competent‑authority monitoring.
– Access to all relevant data for CAMO personnel.
– The CAMO retains the right to override subcontractor recommendations if necessary.
– The subcontractor must maintain qualified technical expertise and sufficient resources.

Summary

The CAMO’s responsibilities cover:

  1. Authority & Leadership – The nominated postholder and the group of persons must have the authority and competence to oversee all maintenance activities.
  2. Human Resources – Adequate staffing and training are documented.
  3. Documentation & Records – Systems for aircraft records, MEL, maintenance programmes, and flight‑hour/cycle recording are defined.
  4. Coordination – All duties are clearly elaborated and coordinated within the organization.
  5. Subcontracting Control – When tasks are outsourced, the CAMO controls the arrangement, ensures compliance, and retains ultimate authority over airworthiness decisions.

These elements together form the core of continuing airworthiness management responsibilities as outlined in the retrieved material.
